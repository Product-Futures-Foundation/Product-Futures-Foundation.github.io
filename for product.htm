=Overview
The For Product model is another way to organize property and work.
Workers and consumers become owners to ensure future production.
Owners in this system avoid paying rent, interest and profit.

=Timeline
Future products are sold to buy sources required to begin production.
Those sources are 'sold' workers for their commitments of future work.
Owners accept the product itself as the natural return on investment.
Surplus is sold, but some profit is treated as the payer's investment.

=Terms
Consumers become source owners when they prepay for future products.
Workers become source owners when they promise to work in the future.
Owners of sources the are owners of future products without purchase.
Consumers become source owners when they buy surplus and pay profit.
