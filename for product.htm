=Overview
Using property ownership to minimize rent, interest and profit.
Workers and consumers become owners to ensure future production.
They buy and own productive inputs because they need the outputs.

=Timeline
Future products are sold to buy sources required to begin production.
These sources are 'sold' to workers who commit to future production.
Owners accept the product itself as the natural return on investment.
Owners sell surplus product but some profit becomes payer investment.

=Terms
0. The owners of sources own that same % of the future products.
1. Consumers become source owners when they buy future products.
2. Workers become source owners when they promise future production.
3. Consumers become source owners when they pay profit on surplus.
